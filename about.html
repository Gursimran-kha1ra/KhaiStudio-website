Meet Pawanpreet

A Photographer Drawn to Weddings

Hi, I'm Pawanpreet, the founder of Khai Studio — a photographer drawn to weddings, especially Indian weddings. I'm not moved by scale or grandeur, but by what exists within them. A father letting go. A mother holding back. Two people beginning something they don't yet understand, but choose anyway.

In Indian weddings, I often find myself between two worlds — the nervous silences before the ceremony, and the loudest cheers after. A wedding, to me, is a balance of stillness and motion. Of what is held close, and what unfolds in between. My work is to preserve these moments as they are felt — quietly, honestly, and without interruption.

If weddings move you the way they move me, we will understand each other.

The Studio

A Studio Built on Story

Crafted with intention. Rooted in Surrey.

A Personal Approach

Khai Studio is based in Surrey, BC, and proudly serves couples and families across the entire Metro Vancouver region. Built on creativity, intention, and a strong belief in storytelling through visuals, the studio offers photography that feels personal — not generic, not rushed.

Each session is approached with care and attention, with a focus on making you feel comfortable and confident in front of the camera.

Photography With Meaning

Whether it's a Mehndi night full of laughter, a quiet first look between two people, or the full arc of a multi-day wedding — the goal stays the same: capture genuine moments and transform them into timeless images.

The focus is not just on how a photo looks, but on how it feels years later when you look back at it.

My Approach

Quietly, Honestly, Without Interruption

Photography is more than images — it's about emotion, energy, and authenticity. I believe the best photos happen when you feel natural, not overly posed.

From the first conversation to the final delivery, my focus is on creating an experience that feels effortless while delivering results that feel meaningful. I photograph across Surrey, Vancouver, Burnaby, Richmond, Langley, and beyond — and travel further when the story calls for it.
